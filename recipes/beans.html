Refried Beans

Description

Refried beans are a staple dish in almost any Mexican household. They are incredibly tasty and packed with protein. Given how easy they are to make, it's no wonder they are so popular even outside of Mexico!

Ingredients

  • Pinto beans
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Yellow onion
  • Garlic
  • Mexican oregano
  • Fat
  • Jalapeño (optional)

Steps

  1. Rinse and clean the beans to discard any small unwanted particles.
  2. In a large pot, combine the beans, yellow onion, cloves of smashed garlic, dried Mexican oregano, salt, and enough water to cover the ingredients plus around 3 inches.
  3. Bring to a boil with medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low, cover with a lid, and let simmer for around 2 hours.
  4. Once the beans are done cooking, strain the water out into a separate bowl.
  5. In a large pot or skillet, add some cooking oil, chopped onion, jalapeño, garlic, Mexican oregano, and salt. Cook for around 5 minutes until the onions are soft.
  6. Add cooked beans to the skillet and cook for a couple of minutes before mixing in ¾ of a cup of the strained bean broth.
  7. Mash the beans using a potato masher for a chunky texture or a blender for a smoother texture.
  8. Add salt if needed, top with crumbled queso fresco or cotija cheese.
